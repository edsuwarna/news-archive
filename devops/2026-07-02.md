## 1. 📡 T-Mobile Moving Tens of Thousands of VMs Off VMware Amid Broadcom Lawsuit
T-Mobile is migrating tens of thousands of virtual machines off VMware amid an ongoing legal dispute with Broadcom over perpetual license support. The carrier wants Broadcom to continue supporting its existing VMware perpetual licenses as it executes a massive re-platforming effort. This is one of the largest VMware-to-alternative migrations in recent memory, with significant implications for enterprise virtualization strategy.
🔗 https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2026/07/t-mobile-moving-tens-of-thousands-of-virtual-machines-off-vmware-amid-lawsuit/

## 2. 🐑 AWS Lambda Introduces MicroVMs for Isolated Sandbox Execution
AWS launched a new serverless compute primitive — Lambda MicroVMs — providing VM-level isolated sandboxes with no shared kernel or resources between sessions. These offer rapid launch and resume, full lifecycle control, state preservation up to 8 hours, and no infrastructure to manage. This is a game-changer for running untrusted agent/user code, ephemeral CI/CD runners, and secure multi-tenant workloads on Lambda.
🔗 https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/run-isolated-sandboxes-with-full-lifecycle-control-aws-lambda-introduces-microvms/

## 3. ☸️ Amazon EKS Now Supports Kubernetes Version Rollbacks
Amazon EKS introduced Kubernetes version rollbacks, letting cluster operators reverse upgrades within a 7-day window. This provides a critical safety net for upgrade failures — no cluster rebuilds required — turning what was once a one-way forward operation into a reversible, low-risk process. A major step forward for production K8s operations.
🔗 https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/upgrade-amazon-eks-clusters-with-confidence-using-kubernetes-version-rollbacks/
