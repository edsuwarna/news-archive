## 1. 🚀 81,920 Cores Per Rack: AMD EPYC "Venice" Debuts at HPE Discover 2026

At HPE Discover 2026, HPE unveiled the Cray GX250a compute blade packing **8x AMD EPYC Venice CPUs** per sled, totaling an eye-watering **81,920 cores per rack**. The Venice platform brings 16-channel memory per CPU, MRDIMMs, and full liquid cooling throughout — even the DIMMs are on the cooling loop. HPE pairs this with its Slingshot 400 interconnect, a 1.6MW CDU, and future Slingshot 800 support. This is density engineering at its finest, aimed squarely at HPC and large-scale AI training deployments that need maximum compute per square foot.

## 2. 🔌 Intel E835 NICs: 200GbE at Half the Power of ConnectX-6

Intel's new E835 NIC family surfaced at HPE Discover 2026, bringing 200GbE connectivity that Intel claims uses **roughly half the power** of NVIDIA's ConnectX-6 Dx (launched in 2019). The lineup includes dual-port 100GbE, single-port 200GbE, and quad-port 25GbE variants — all in low-profile form factors. With recommended pricing **sub-$600 for 200GbE cards** and sub-$216 for 25GbE, Intel is targeting cost-conscious server deployments that don't need bleeding-edge 800GbE AI fabric but still want modern, efficient connectivity.

## 3. 🧠 Tensordyne Napier: A 3nm AI Processor Built on Logarithmic Math

Tensordyne announced Napier, a 3nm TSMC AI inference processor that replaces conventional matrix multipliers with **logarithmic arithmetic** — turning multiplications into additions for dramatically smaller silicon area. The TDN72 rack-scale system packs 72 accelerators, 68 petaflops, and 42TB of HBM, targeting **1,300 tokens/second for 2-trillion-parameter MoE models** at just 120kW. Tensordyne claims this beats NVIDIA (9 racks, 1.5MW) and AWS+Cerebras (14 racks, 800kW) for the same workload. The chip is taped out with a 2027 system roadmap — big claims that now need to survive real-world deployment.

**Source:** https://www.servethehome.com/tensordyne-napier-ai-processor-announced-with-logarithmic-math/

## 5. 🖥️ Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances with Full HW Control

Lambda announced **Bare Metal Instances** for its GPU cloud, starting with NVIDIA GB300 NVL72 Superclusters. These give teams direct, unmediated access to CPU, memory, GPU, disk, and TPM — **no hypervisor, no third-party virtualization layer**. ## 6. ⚡ Lambda Tops MLPerf v6.0 on GB300 NVL72 and HGX B200

Lambda posted the **fastest Llama 3.1 8B training time on GB300 NVL72** (11.59 minutes, beating NVIDIA's own 11.75 min) and the **fastest MoE training on HGX B200** (96.46 minutes for GPT-OSS-20B) in MLPerf Training v6.0 — the first round to include Mixture-of-Experts benchmarks. The gains come from NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra innovations (279GB HBM3e, higher bandwidth, improved interconnect) combined with Lambda's cluster design and tuned NeMo stack. Eight teams submitted GB300 NVL72 results this round: Lambda, Nebius, CoreWeave, Oracle, Dell, GigaComputing, HPE, and NVIDIA — showing the GPU BMaaS ecosystem is heating up fast.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/mlperf-training-v6.0-lambda-delivers-fastest-llm-training

## 7. 🔦 First Look: NVIDIA's Co-Packaged Optics Switch Unboxed by Lambda

Lambda unboxed one of NVIDIA's first **co-packaged optics (CPO) InfiniBand switches** — the Quantum-X Photonics Q3450-LD. The numbers are striking: a CPO switch draws **3.95 kW vs 7.0 kW** for a standard switch, saving 3.05 kW per unit. In a 41,472-GPU cluster, CPO saves **4.4 MW** — equivalent to adding +3,137 GPUs within the same power envelope. CPO also eliminates 655,000 discrete transceiver modules from a 128K-GPU cluster, removing that many failure points. The switch is 4U, liquid-cooled, with 115.2 Tb/s non-blocking capacity. This is a glimpse of where AI fabric networking is heading.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda

## 10. 🍋 AMD Lemonade 10.8: MCP Server Integration + Experimental GB10 ARM64 Support

AMD's open-source Lemonade AI server v10.8 adds **Model Context Protocol (MCP) server integration**, letting any MCP-compatible client (GitHub Copilot, Claude Desktop, Cursor) use locally-running models as tools — including chat, audio transcription, and image generation. Notably, v10.8 adds **experimental NVIDIA GB10 ARM64 support** via the Llama.cpp CUDA back-end, hinting at broader ARM server AI inference. For bare-metal AI infrastructure operators, Lemonade provides a free, private AI serving layer that runs across AMD NPUs, Radeon GPUs, and x86 CPUs — no cloud dependency required.

## 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **Ultra-dense compute is accelerating** — AMD EPYC Venice pushing 81,920 cores/rack with full liquid cooling shows the race for rack-level compute density is far from over. Expect 100K+ core racks within 12 months.

- **BMaaS is going mainstream** — Lambda's bare metal instances, now with API-driven provisioning and TPM security, signal that GPU BMaaS is maturing beyond "rent a server" into a genuine cloud alternative. MLPerf results show BMaaS providers competing with hyper scalers on performance.

- **Networking is the new bottleneck** — From Intel's power-efficient 200GbE NICs to NVIDIA's CPO switches and HPE's Juniper integration, the industry is racing to build network infrastructure that doesn't starve GPUs. CPO's 3+kW savings per switch will be a key differentiator in 2027 cluster designs.

- **ARM server CPUs gain real contenders** — NVIDIA Vera benchmarks vs Ampere Altra show the ARM server ecosystem now has two serious high-performance players. Combined with AMD and Intel's continued advances, the server CPU market has more competition than it's seen in a decade.

- **AI inference drives new hardware paradigms** — Tensordyne's logarithmic math processor and AMD's Lemonade MCP integration both point to a future where AI inference hardware and software are increasingly specialized, disaggregated from training infrastructure, and optimized for token-per-watt economics.
