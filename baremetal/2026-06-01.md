## 1. 🔥 ASUS XA NB3I-E12: A 9U Monster Packing 8× NVIDIA B300 GPUs
**ServeTheHome** published a full teardown of ASUS's massive 9U GPU server built around the NVIDIA HGX B300 platform. The system packs 8 NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra B300 GPUs, Intel Xeon processors, and over 6.4 Tbps of networking (8× ConnectX-8 800G XDR InfiniBand per GPU) — all in an **air-cooled chassis** designed to fit into existing racks and data centers without liquid cooling retrofits. It's a clear signal that the B300 generation is pushing the boundaries of what air cooling can handle.
→ https://www.servethehome.com/asus-xa-nb3i-e12-review-a-massive-8x-nvidia-b300-gpu-server/

## 2. 🚀 Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances — No Hypervisor, Full API Control

The instance lifecycle is fully API-driven, blending the performance and security of bare-metal with cloud-grade usability. This bridges the gap for quantitative traders, frontier labs, and large-scale inference teams who need both hardware control and operational simplicity.
→ https://lambda.ai/blog/lambda-bare-metal-instances

A strong alternative to AMD EPYC in the compact server space.

Xsight's E1 is positioning itself as a serious contender against NVIDIA BlueField and Intel IPU in the ARM DPU/SmartNIC space.

## 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🏋️‍♂️ GPU Bare-Metal Goes Gigawatt-Scale**: Lambda's $1B credit facility and ASUS's 9U B300 monster show that AI infrastructure is driving unprecedented density and capital deployment in the bare-metal space. The B300/HGX generation is pushing power and cooling to the limit.
- **❄️ Liquid Cooling Becomes Table Stakes**: Equinix and others are racing to retrofit colocation facilities for direct-to-chip liquid cooling. Air-cooled 9U B300 systems exist but are the exception — the industry is shifting to liquid for anything beyond 50kW/rack.
- **🔄 Cloud Repatriation Is Real**: Equinix's "Cloud Isn't Right for Everything" messaging and Lambda's Bare Metal Instances both signal that enterprises are re-evaluating the all-in cloud bet. Bare-metal and colocation are having a renaissance for AI and latency-sensitive workloads.
- **🔧 ARM in the Data Center Gets Practical**: The Xsight Labs E1 running standard Ubuntu LTS out of the box is a milestone for ARM server adoption. ARM DPUs, SmartNICs, and server SoCs are moving from experimental to production-ready.
- **📉 Pricing Transparency Tools Are Emerging**: Projects like gpuhunt (open-source pricing comparison) signal that the bare-metal GPU market is maturing — when there's enough provider competition to need a comparison tool, the market has arrived.
