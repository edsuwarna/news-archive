# Bare-Metal Server News — June 7, 2026

Research compiled from ServeTheHome, Phoronix, Lambda Labs, Equinix Blog, and The Register.

## 1. 🚀 Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances with NVIDIA GB300 NVL72

**Lambda** has officially launched **Bare Metal Instances** — a new product category that gives customers direct hardware access (CPU, GPU, memory, disk, TPM) combined with API-driven cloud operations. No hypervisor, no abstraction layer. Launching first on Superclusters with the NVIDIA GB300 NVL72 rack-scale system, these instances use NVIDIA BlueField DPUs operating in zero-trust mode to handle provisioning, network identity, and storage volumes. The architecture solves the classic trade-off between bare-metal control and cloud usability — and opens the door to even larger systems like Vera Rubin NVL72.

> *"The unit of AI compute has shifted from single hosts to rack-scale systems."*

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/lambda-bare-metal-instances

## 2. 🏛️ New York Advances One-Year Datacenter Permit Moratorium

**New York lawmakers** have approved a bill that would impose a **one-year moratorium on permits for datacenters drawing 20 MW or more**, along with new labor, energy, environmental, and community-benefit requirements. The bill — passed by both the Assembly and Senate — now heads to Governor Kathy Hochul for signature. It includes a phased mandate for renewable energy (33% by 2030, 90% by 2040) and requires datacenters to foot the bill for grid upgrades. Business groups including Tech:NYC are urging Hochul to veto, arguing the rushed legislation would strangulate AI infrastructure investment. Early drafts had called for a three-year pause.

**Source:** https://www.theregister.com/legal/2026/06/05/new-york-advances-one-year-datacenter-permit-moratorium/5251911

## 3. 🔥 NVIDIA Co-Packaged Optics Switch Unboxed by Lambda

**Lambda** has published a detailed first look at the **NVIDIA Quantum-X InfiniBand Photonics Q3450-LD** — one of the first co-packaged optics (CPO) switches in production. CPO integrates optics directly into the switch package, dramatically reducing power consumption: at 800G and GB300 NVL72 scale, the back-end fabric accounts for 86% of networking power in a three-layer cluster. As agentic AI workloads drive more east-west traffic inside GPU clusters, CPO networking becomes critical for maintaining token throughput. This is a major hardware infrastructure play for next-gen AI factories.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda

## 5. 📈 NVIDIA Vera ARM Server CPU: 7–15x Performance Leap in 8 Years

**Phoronix** published a retrospective benchmark comparing **ARM server CPUs from 2018 (Ampere eMAG) to NVIDIA Vera** — and the numbers are staggering: more than **7x geomean improvement in 8 years**, with some workloads hitting nearly **15x faster**. The Vera CPU delivers the fastest ARM server performance Phoronix has ever tested. This puts real data behind the ARM-in-the-datacenter narrative and shows the pace of architectural improvement far outstripping x86's generational gains in the same period.

**Source:** https://www.phoronix.com/review/nvidia-vera-arm-server

## 6. 🏭 AMD EPYC 8635P "Sorano" Benchmarks: Major Upgrade Path for EPYC 8004 Servers

**Phoronix** benchmarks the **AMD EPYC 8635P (Sorano)** — the flagship 84-core Zen 5 processor in the EPYC 8005 series. These are **drop-in upgrades for existing EPYC 8004 (Siena) servers** after a BIOS update, ranging from 8 to 84 cores. The 8005 series targets single-socket, power-efficient servers, making it an attractive option for edge and mid-range bare-metal deployments. Performance gains over the previous generation are significant across compute, HPC, and data center workloads.

**Source:** https://www.phoronix.com/review/amd-epyc-8635p

## 7. 🧊 Intel Xeon 7 "Diamond Rapids" Delayed to 2027

**Intel's Xeon 7** series, codenamed **"Diamond Rapids"**, is now expected to arrive in **2027**, pushing back the next major server CPU generation. This leaves Intel's data center roadmap in an increasingly crowded and competitive space — with AMD EPYC Turin/Sorano already shipping (Zen 5), NVIDIA Vera (ARM) surging, and Qualcomm Dragonfly entering the fray. The delay raises questions about Intel's ability to reclaim process and architecture leadership in the server segment.

**Source:** https://www.servethehome.com/intel-xeon-7-diamond-rapids-now-slated-for-2027/

## 8. 🌊 Equinix: The Anatomy of a Direct-to-Chip Liquid Cooling System

**Equinix** published a deep technical breakdown of **direct-to-chip liquid cooling** for modern datacenters. As GPU power densities hit **200+ kW per rack** (trending toward 1 MW), traditional air cooling is no longer viable. The article walks through the entire cooling loop: coolant distribution units (CDUs), cold plates mounted directly on chips, facility water connections, and the monitoring/control infrastructure. This is essential reading for anyone operating or planning bare-metal GPU infrastructure at scale.

**Source:** https://blog.equinix.com/blog/2026/05/07/the-anatomy-of-a-direct-to-chip-liquid-cooling-system/

## 9. 🤖 Equinix: Agentic AI Is Changing the AI Hardware Equation

**Equinix** argues that the next generation of AI workloads — **agent-driven, multi-step, constantly in motion** — isn't limited by compute capacity but by how effectively compute, data, and connectivity are coordinated globally. Enterprises who bought GPUs as if capacity alone would solve AI are now hitting a different constraint: distributed inference requires globally interconnected infrastructure, not isolated GPU clusters. This shifts the hardware equation from "how many GPUs" to "how are they interconnected across locations."

**Source:** https://blog.equinix.com/blog/2026/05/12/agentic-ai-is-changing-the-ai-hardware-equation/

## 🔮 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **BMaaS hits prime time** 🔥 — Lambda's Bare Metal Instances mark a shift: API-driven lifecycle on bare metal, no hypervisor, using DPU/BMC offload. Expect AWS, Azure, and GCP to respond with more bare-metal SKUs for AI workloads.
- **DC regulation wave is building** 🌊 — New York's datacenter moratorium (following Ohio's pause on tax breaks) signals growing pushback on AI infrastructure's energy footprint. More states and countries will follow — expect this to affect bare-metal deployment timelines.
- **ARM server momentum is real** 🚀 — Between NVIDIA Vera's 7-15x performance leap, Qualcomm Dragonfly entering the market, and AMD Zen 5's continued dominance, the server CPU market is the most competitive it's been in a decade. Intel Diamond Rapids slipping to 2027 doesn't help their position.
- **Liquid cooling becomes table stakes** 🌡️ — 200+ kW per rack and trending toward 1 MW means air cooling is dead for GPU clusters. Direct-to-chip liquid cooling is now a deployment prerequisite for any serious bare-metal AI infrastructure.
- **Networking is the new bottleneck** 🔗 — Co-packaged optics (NVIDIA Q3450-LD) and distributed AI architectures are forcing a re-think: bare-metal is no longer about individual servers but about interconnected rack-scale systems where the network fabric is as important as the compute.
