### 1. 🚀 Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances — DPU-Powered BMaaS for NVIDIA GB300 NVL72

Lambda unveiled **Bare Metal Instances** running on NVIDIA GB300 NVL72 Superclusters, giving customers direct, unmediated access to CPU, memory, GPU, disk, and TPM — with no third-party hypervisor. The architecture uses NVIDIA BlueField DPUs in zero-trust mode for provisioning and BMCs for out-of-band health monitoring, creating a fully API-driven instance lifecycle. This is a major evolution of BMaaS: cloud-grade APIs without sacrificing bare-metal performance or security.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/lambda-bare-metal-instances

### 2. 🔥 Gigabyte's 40-Node 1U Cluster Packs 320 Cores & 80 SSDs — Extreme Density at Computex 2026

At Computex 2026, Gigabyte showed off the **R1C7-K0A-AS1** — a 1U chassis holding 40 nodes based on Intel Core Ultra 7 258V (Lunar Lake), each with 32GB LPDDR5X and dual M.2 SSDs. That's **320 cores, 1.28TB memory, 80 SSDs, and 40 integrated GPUs in a single 1U**. Scale that across a 42U rack and you're looking at 12,800 CPU cores, 3,200 SSDs, and 51.2TB of memory. The system uses 3.2kW Titanium PSUs and consolidates connectivity to two QSFP28 ports per chassis.

### 4. 🔌 NVIDIA's First Co-Packaged Optics Switch Unboxed by Lambda — Saves 3kW Per Switch

Lambda unboxed and installed the **NVIDIA Quantum-X InfiniBand Photonics Q3450-LD**, one of NVIDIA's first co-packaged optics (CPO) switches. Each CPO switch draws **3.95 kW vs. 7.0 kW** for standard pluggable transceivers — saving **3.05 kW per switch**. In a 41,472-GPU cluster, that frees **4.4 MW of power**, the equivalent of running 3,137 extra GPUs. The CPO switch also eliminates ~655,000 discrete transceiver modules, massively reducing fabric failure points. 4U form factor, liquid-cooled via UDQ4 connectors, 144 × 800G InfiniBand ports, 115.2 Tb/s non-blocking.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda

### 5. 🔓 AMD openSIL + Coreboot Runs on EPYC — Open-Source Server Firmware Milestone

Phoronix published the first full review of **AMD's openSIL (open-source silicon initialization) running with Coreboot on an EPYC server**. This replaces AMD's proprietary AGESA firmware with fully auditable, open-source initialization code — a watershed moment for bare-metal security, supply chain transparency, and platform customization. Since AMD announced openSIL in early 2023, this is the first time it's been validated end-to-end on production EPYC hardware.

**Source:** https://www.phoronix.com/review/amd-opensil-coreboot-ar1

## 📈 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🔷 BMaaS enters the DPU era** — Lambda's Bare Metal Instances prove that the next frontier of bare-metal cloud is DPU-driven provisioning combined with API-first lifecycle management, eliminating the classic trade-off between hardware control and cloud convenience
- **🔷 Co-packaged optics is becoming real** — NVIDIA's CPO switch unboxing by Lambda shows the industry moving beyond pluggable transceivers. With 3kW+ savings per switch and dramatically fewer failure points, CPO will shape GPU cluster fabric design for the next 5 years
- **🔷 Open-source firmware gains ground in servers** — AMD openSIL + Coreboot on EPYC is a practical milestone. Bare-metal operators increasingly demand auditable, transparent firmware — and vendors are starting to deliver
- **🔷 Extreme compute density is accelerating** — Gigabyte's 40-node 1U Lunar Lake cluster shows that high-density edge/compute clusters can pack 12,800 cores per rack. This is a blueprint for CDN edge, transcoding farms, and VDI at scale
- **🔷 AI infrastructure investment shows no signs of slowing** — Lambda's $1B credit facility (4× upsized) and HRT's move to external bare-metal GPU both confirm that organizations are betting big on GPU bare-metal cloud over self-built data centers for AI workloads
