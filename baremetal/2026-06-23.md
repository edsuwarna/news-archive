## 📊 Bare-Metal Trend Summary

- **🔵 GPU BMaaS enters its DPU-powered era** — Lambda's Bare Metal Instances prove that DPU-based provisioning can deliver real cloud-class bare-metal experiences. Expect CoreWeave, Azure, and GCP to follow with copycat offerings that decouple lifecycle management from hypervisor overhead.

- **🔵 ARM server CPUs are now a three-horse race** — NVIDIA Vera's benchmarking against Ampere Altra Max, plus AMD EPYC Venice dominance, confirms ARM BMaaS is mainstream. Three ARM server architectures (Vera, Ampere, Graviton) now compete with x86 for production workloads.

- **🔵 Co-packaged optics will reshape GPU cluster economics** — CPO switches saving 3.05 kW per unit are not incremental improvements; they change the power budget math for 10,000+ GPU clusters. This is the networking story to watch for 2027 facility planning.

- **🔵 Liquid cooling is becoming operational standard, not future tech** — From HPE's liquid-cooled EPYC Venice racks to MiTAC's diamond cooling, the question is no longer "if" data centers will adopt liquid cooling but "which method." Direct-to-chip, immersion, and diamond-TIM are all in production now.

- **🔵 MLPerf is now the BMaaS competitive benchmark** — 8 teams submitting GB300 NVL72 results in MLPerf v6.0 creates a transparent, standardized comparison for GPU bare-metal performance. Expect providers to compete aggressively on training benchmarks as the market matures.
