## 📊 Bare-Metal Trend Summary

- **Density is accelerating fast** — HPE's 81,920 cores per rack with AMD EPYC Venice + liquid cooling shows that the race for per-rack compute density is far from over. Expect 300kW+ racks to become standard for HPC/AI.
- **Bare-metal as a service is maturing** — Lambda's bare-metal instances with BlueField DPUs represent a shift toward API-driven, no-hypervisor infrastructure for AI workloads. BMaaS is becoming the default for frontier AI.
- **Networking is the new bottleneck** — With CPO optics and 800G fabrics, the network now accounts for 86% of cluster power. Infrastructure teams need to think about networking as seriously as compute.
- **ARM servers are gaining momentum** — NVIDIA Vera vs Ampere Altra benchmarks show real generational progress in ARM server CPUs. The ARM bare-metal ecosystem is diversifying beyond Graviton.
- **Agentic AI drives CPU demand too** — The narrative is all about GPUs, but agentic AI workloads are creating a new wave of dense CPU server deployments for planning, reasoning, and tool execution loops.
