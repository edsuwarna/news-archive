## 6. 🔬 Lambda Unboxes One of NVIDIA's First Co-Packaged Optics Switches (CPO)

Lambda got early hands-on with NVIDIA's Quantum-X InfiniBand Photonics Q3450-LD switch, the first co-packaged optics (CPO) switch in their GPU cluster fleet. At 800G speeds and GB300 NVL72 scale, the back-end fabric accounts for 86% of networking power in a three-layer cluster. CPO dramatically cuts power draw and failure points — a major networking breakthrough for bare-metal AI infrastructure.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda

## 7. 🎯 Why Your Kubernetes Scheduler Can't Handle AI Workloads

Lambda dives deep into the GPU scheduling crisis on bare-metal Kubernetes clusters. The default `kube-scheduler` lacks gang scheduling and multi-node fabric topology awareness, causing partial-scheduling deadlocks and severe communication latency for distributed training across dozens or hundreds of GPUs. Alternatives like Kueue, KAI Scheduler, and Volcano are explored — essential reading for anyone running K8s on bare-metal for AI.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/why-your-kubernetes-scheduler-cant-handle-ai-workloads

## 🔮 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **Edge AI drives dedicated hardware diversification** — NVIDIA's Thor platform expansion (T3000/T2000) and ASRock Rack's edge server prove that bare-metal at the edge is a growing market beyond traditional x86.
- **GPU bare-metal clusters are hitting new performance peaks** — Lambda's MLPerf results on GB300 NVL72 and HGX B200 show that purpose-built GPU infrastructure continues to outpace general-purpose cloud for AI training.
- **Networking is becoming the bottleneck, and CPO is the answer** — Co-packaged optics switches tackle the power and reliability crisis in large GPU clusters. At 800G scale, the network fabric consumes 86% of networking power — CPO slashes that.
- **Kubernetes on bare-metal has a GPU scheduling problem** — Default schedulers can't handle distributed training at scale. Gang scheduling and fabric-topology-aware placement are becoming must-haves for AI infrastructure platforms.
- **Colocation is strategic again** — Equinix's positioning on hybrid multicloud, heat reuse, and AI infrastructure scaling shows that bare-metal colocation is not dying — it's evolving as the foundation for AI workloads, with enterprises balancing scale-up (neoclouds) and scale-out (distributed AI).
