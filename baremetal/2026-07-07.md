## 3. 🔌 NVIDIA Quantum-X Photonics CPO Switch Unboxed — 3.05 kW Saved Per Switch

**Summary:** Lambda unboxed one of NVIDIA's first co-packaged optics InfiniBand switches (Q3450-LD), revealing a 3.05 kW power savings per switch vs traditional pluggable transceivers. In a 41,472-GPU cluster, that frees ~4.4 MW — equivalent to +3,137 GPUs of power headroom. The switch uses external light-source modules feeding 144 MPO fiber-array ports, liquid-cooled via UDQ4 quick disconnects, in a 4U form factor. CPO eliminates an entire component failure class (655K discrete transceivers in a 128K-GPU cluster), directly impacting GPU cluster reliability and TCO.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda

## 4. 💾 Micron Shows First PCIe Gen6 Data Center SSD (9650) at Computex 2026

**Summary:** Ahead of PCIe Gen6 CPU launches, Micron demonstrated its first Gen6 enterprise SSD, the 9650, at Computex 2026. A clear signal that the storage silicon pipeline is ready for next-gen server platforms.

## 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🥇 GPU BMaaS is entering its DPU-powered era.** Lambda's Bare Metal Instances (BlueField DPUs + BMC orchestration) represent a fundamental shift from IPMI/PXE-driven dedicated servers to API-native, cloud-class bare-metal provisioning. Expect copycat offerings from CoreWeave, Nebius, and others in the next 6 months.

- **🔌 Co-packaged optics are the biggest bare-metal networking story of 2026.** The NVIDIA Q3450-LD CPO switch saves 3.05 kW per unit — that's real TCO for anyone building GPU clusters at scale. CPO removes ~655K discrete failure points (transceivers) from a 128K-GPU fabric. This isn't theoretical anymore; it's being installed in production racks.

- **⚡ MLPerf is now the de facto GPU BMaaS benchmark.** Eight teams submitted GB300 NVL72 results in v6.0. If you're comparing bare-metal GPU providers, MLPerf Training times are the most valuable apples-to-apples data point available. Lambda, CoreWeave, Oracle, Dell, HPE, and NVIDIA themselves are all on the leaderboard.

- **🦾 ARM server CPU competition has reached critical mass.** Qualcomm Dragonfly C1000, ASRock Rack's AGI server, NVIDIA Vera (benchmarked vs Ampere Altra Max and GB10), ARM #1 on Top500 — and now Canonical making Ubuntu ARM64 first-class. ARM bare-metal is no longer niche; it's a multicloud diversification strategy.

- **💾 PCIe Gen6 storage is prepped and waiting on Gen6 CPUs.** Micron's 9650 SSD and Silicon Motion's SM8008/SM8388 controllers are ready. The storage industry is building Gen6 capacity ahead of server CPU availability. When Granite Rapids and Diamond Rapids ship with Gen6 support, bare-metal storage performance will leap overnight.
