### 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **Gen6 storage is here before Gen6 CPUs** — Samsung and Micron are shipping PCIe Gen6 SSDs, but Intel/AMD Gen6 platforms haven't launched yet. Storage infrastructure is leapfrogging compute, forcing bare-metal architects to think about I/O bottlenecks differently.
- **ARM server CPUs are accelerating on two fronts** — AWS Graviton5 delivers 30% generational gains, while NVIDIA's Rosa/Rigel architecture promises high-core-count ARM for AI-optimized bare-metal. The x86 duopoly is genuinely under pressure for the first time in a decade.
- **GPU BMaaS is now a measurable market** — With 8+ teams submitting GB300 NVL72 results in MLPerf v6.0, the GPU bare-metal rental market has transparent benchmarks. Competition is shifting from "who has GPUs" to "who trains faster per dollar."
- **Network power is the new battleground** — CPO switches (Lambda/NVIDIA) saving 3kW per switch signals that datacenter interconnect power draw is now a first-class constraint in bare-metal cluster design.
- **Mainframes and edge converge** — IBM's rackmount LinuxONE Express and AMD's Ryzen AI Halo demonstrate that bare-metal is bifurcating: mainframe-class reliability shrinks down, while AI-capable edge hardware scales up. Both are responses to the same cloud repatriation push.
