## 3. 🧠 Intel Expands Memory Options for Xeon 6 & Xeon 6+ Servers Mid-Cycle

For bare-metal operators, this means more memory bandwidth without forklift upgrades.

## 🔥 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **Memory bandwidth race is on:** With DDR5-8000 RDIMMs and MRDIMM Gen2 hitting 12,800 MT/s, the server memory landscape is transforming faster than any time in the last decade. Intel, AMD, and memory vendors all pushing simultaneous upgrades mid-cycle — great news for bare-metal operators looking to squeeze more from existing platforms without full platform refreshes.

- **NVIDIA is becoming a full-stack bare-metal player:** Between Vera CPU architectural disclosures, GB300 NVL72 MLPerf dominance, and the continued success of their GPU compute platforms, NVIDIA is no longer just an accelerator vendor — they're architecting complete server platforms that compete directly with AMD and Intel in the bare-metal data center.

- **Liquid cooling is moving from niche to standard:** MSI showing DLC on a 1OU server node signals that liquid cooling is becoming table stakes for next-gen CPU platforms. AMD Venice, Intel Diamond Rapids, and NVIDIA's high-power GB300 systems will all push thermal envelopes that demand advanced cooling — bare-metal operators should be planning data center cooling roadmaps now.

- **Colocation demand driven by AI sovereignty:** Equinix HK6 and the broader trend of AI-specific colocation facilities highlight that geopolitical factors (data sovereignty, cross-border regulations) are increasingly driving bare-metal infrastructure decisions, not just pure cost/compute calculus.

- **Custom silicon for dedicated appliances is having a moment:** Intel Foundry's ASIC win with Fortinet, combined with the proliferation of GPU accelerators and DPUs, shows that the dedicated hardware market for bare-metal security, networking, and compute appliances is vibrant and growing.
