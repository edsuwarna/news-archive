**📋 BARE METAL TREND SUMMARY**

• 🚀 **BMaaS is going mainstream** — Lambda's Bare Metal Instances launch (no hypervisor, API-driven) signals that bare-metal cloud is becoming a standard offering alongside virtualized cloud, especially for AI/ML workloads that need deterministic performance.

• 🏭 **AI infrastructure spending is exploding** — Lambda's $1B credit facility and $275M expansion within a year show the capital intensity of GPU bare-metal cloud. Expect more consolidation in the BMaaS GPU market as smaller players struggle to keep up with infrastructure costs.

• 🔧 **Qualcomm enters the data center** — The Dragonfly brand launch marks a third major ARM server player (alongside Ampere and AWS Graviton). If Qualcomm follows through, x86 dominance in dedicated servers faces another credible challenger. Watch the June 24th announcement closely.

• ❄️ **Liquid cooling is no longer optional** — Equinix's deep dive on DTC cooling and Lambda's CPO switch deployment confirm that liquid cooling is now table stakes for any facility hosting high-density GPU clusters. Colo operators without liquid cooling capability will lose the AI workload market.

• 💻 **Desktop AI hardware is converging with server tech** — Microsoft's Surface RTX Spark and AMD's Ryzen AI Halo show that AI development hardware is becoming a new category. These aren't bare-metal servers, but they represent the lower end of a continuum where the same NVIDIA GPUs power everything from dev PCs to 72-GPU superclusters.

• 📡 **Co-packaged optics signal the next networking revolution** — NVIDIA's CPO switch previewed by Lambda suggests that the next-generation AI data center fabric will be optically switched at the board level, dramatically cutting power and latency in the network layer — directly impacting how bare-metal clusters are interconnected.

• 🗓️ **Server CPU roadmap extends into 2027** — Intel's Diamond Rapids being pushed to 2027 and AMD's Zen 6 tuning in GCC compilers tell us that traditional server CPU upgrade cycles remain multi-year. For bare-metal operators, this means platforms deployed today need to stay relevant for 3-4 years of AI workload evolution.
