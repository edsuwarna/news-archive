### 2. 🔬 IBM Outlines Sub-1nm Nanostack Transistor Technology

IBM unveils its **Nanostack transistor architecture** aimed at sub-1nm chip fabrication in the 2030s. Rather than shrinking transistors horizontally, nanostack builds them **vertically via wafer stacking** — a paradigm shift that could extend Moore's Law into the next decade. For bare-metal operators, this means more transistors per mm², lower power-per-core, and denser compute blades in future server generations.

**Source:** [ServeTheHome](https://www.servethehome.com/ibm-outlines-sub-1nm-nanostack-transistor-technology/)

## 📈 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🥇 ARM supremacy in HPC** — Arm-based systems now occupy #1 on the Top500. ARM server CPU competition is white-hot between NVIDIA (Vera/GB10), Qualcomm (Dragonfly C1000), Ampere, and Chinese designs. Bare-metal operators have more CPU architecture choices than ever.
- **🧊 Cooling innovation accelerates** — Diamond cooling, 52U racks, and co-packaged optics all point to the industry's struggle with thermal density. Liquid cooling is no longer optional for high-performance GPU clusters.
- **🏭 AI hardware goes custom** — OpenAI's Jalapeño + Broadcom, NVIDIA's full-stack push, and Qualcomm's HBM alternative all signal a shift from off-the-shelf GPUs to purpose-built AI silicon. Bare-metal providers must adapt to heterogeneous hardware.
- **🔄 Cloud repatriation is real** — Rising cloud costs and data sovereignty requirements are driving workloads back to on-prem and colocation. Bare-metal and dedicated server demand is climbing for predictable, high-utilization workloads.
- **🔗 Networking is the new bottleneck** — CPO InfiniBand switches and HBM alternatives show that interconnects are now the critical path for scaling AI clusters. Bare-metal deployments will increasingly differentiate on network architecture.
