# 🖥️ Top 10 Bare-Metal Server News — June 3, 2026

Compiled from ServeTheHome, Phoronix, Lambda Labs, Equinix Blog, and The Register.

## 1. 🚀 Intel Xeon 6+ Clearwater Forest is Here — 288 Cores on 18A

Intel officially launched the Xeon 6900E+ series (Clearwater Forest) at Computex 2026. This is Intel's first 2nm-class Xeon built on the Intel 18A process, packing up to **288 efficiency cores** per socket. The new lineup brings faster memory support (MRDIMM), new packaging, enhanced core designs, and more cache — all compatible with existing platforms. This is a massive leap for dedicated server operators looking for density and power efficiency in their hardware deployments.

## 2. 🏭 NVIDIA Vera Rubin Now in Full Production — Next-Gen GPU for AI Workloads

For the bare-metal crowd, this means serious GPU compute nodes built around Vera Rubin will be hitting the market in the coming quarters — critical for anyone running dedicated GPU infrastructure for AI training and inference.

## 3. 🔮 Intel Xeon 7 Diamond Rapids Slated for 2027 — 192 Cores, No Hyperthreading

Intel's next-next-gen server platform, **Xeon 7 "Diamond Rapids"** , is now officially targeting a 2027 launch. The platform is expected to push core counts to **192 cores** and will reportedly drop Hyperthreading — a significant architectural shift for server workloads. Linux enablement is already underway, with EDAC driver changes being readied for the kernel. This is the play for bare-metal operators who plan their hardware refresh cycles years in advance.

**Source:** https://www.servethehome.com/intel-xeon-7-diamond-rapids-now-slated-for-2027/

## 4. 💻 Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances — Full Hardware Control via API

Lambda Labs unveiled **Lambda Bare Metal Instances**, giving users complete hardware control with API-driven provisioning. This is a direct BMaaS (Bare Metal as a Service) play — you get a dedicated physical server with no hypervisor, full root access, and the ability to manage it programmatically. For AI/ML teams that need raw performance without noisy neighbors but want cloud-like orchestration, this bridges the gap between colocation and cloud.

**Source:** https://lambda.ai/blog/lambda-bare-metal-instances

## 7. 🤖 Agentic AI Is Changing the AI Hardware Equation

Equinix argues that the rise of **agentic AI** (autonomous AI agents that chain tasks) is reshaping hardware requirements. Enterprises that bought GPUs assuming capacity alone was the answer are hitting a new bottleneck: latency, interconnect bandwidth, and inference-optimized architectures. For bare-metal operators, this means the conversation is shifting from "how many GPUs" to "how are they interconnected" — putting networking and memory architecture front and center.

**Source:** https://blog.equinix.com/blog/2026/05/12/agentic-ai-is-changing-the-ai-hardware-equation/

## 8. 🌐 Marvell Computex Keynote: AI Data Center Connectivity at 1.024 Tbps

Marvell's Computex 2026 keynote focused on the need for **massive connectivity in AI data centers**. The company unveiled switch silicon capable of **1.024 Tbps** per port with high radix, low latency, and low power — exactly what AI workloads demand. For bare-metal deployments, the interconnect between nodes is becoming as critical as the compute itself. This is a signal that network hardware is catching up to GPU density.

**Source:** https://www.servethehome.com/marvell-computex-2026-keynote-live-coverage/

## 9. 🐧 Intel Diamond Rapids EDAC & Linux Prep Signals Server Readiness

Phoronix reports that Intel's engineers are actively preparing the Linux kernel for **Diamond Rapids**, including EDAC (Error Detection and Correction) driver changes. The fact that kernel patches are landing nearly a year before launch means the platform is well along in its development cycle. For bare-metal Linux shops, early enablement in mainline is a strong signal that Day-1 support will be solid.

**Source:** https://www.phoronix.com/news/Intel-Diamond-Rapids-EDAC-RRL

## 10. 🏛️ Colocation vs. Cloud for Financial Services — The Pendulum Swings Back

Equinix examines how **financial institutions are reassessing colocation vs. cloud** a decade after the initial cloud migration wave. Low latency, data sovereignty, and regulatory compliance are driving some workloads back to dedicated bare-metal in colocation facilities. The takeaway: cloud isn't the endgame for everyone — hybrid strategies that include bare-metal colocation are making a strong comeback in regulated industries.

**Source:** https://blog.equinix.com/blog/2026/05/06/colocation-vs-cloud-for-financial-services/

## 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **AI is the dominant driver** — Nearly every story this cycle ties back to AI/ML workloads reshaping hardware requirements, from GPU density to interconnect and cooling.
- **Server CPU race heats up** — Intel's 288-core Xeon 6+ is shipping now, and Diamond Rapids (192 cores, 2027) is already in kernel prep. Intel is betting big on core count over clock speed.
- **BMaaS is maturing** — Lambda's bare metal instance launch + $1B infrastructure fund shows the BMaaS model is scaling beyond niche use cases into mainstream AI infrastructure.
- **Cooling is the new battleground** — Direct-to-chip liquid cooling is moving from experimental to essential as power densities in racks continue to climb.
- **Networking is unbottlenecking** — Marvell's 1.024 Tbps switch silicon and NVIDIA's CPO optics signal that the network fabric is evolving fast to keep pace with compute density.
- **Colocation is making a comeback** — Regulatory pressure and AI latency requirements are driving enterprises back toward dedicated hardware in colo facilities, especially in finance.
