**5. 🔌 Lambda Unboxes NVIDIA's First Co-Packaged Optics InfiniBand Switch — 3.05 kW Saved Per Unit**
Lambda published a detailed unboxing of the NVIDIA Quantum-X Photonics Q3450-LD, one of the first co-packaged optics (CPO) InfiniBand switches. CPO technology eliminates pluggable transceivers by integrating optics directly into the switch package, saving 3.05 kW per switch and reducing failure points. In a 41,472-GPU cluster, this translates to ~4.4 MW in power savings — a massive efficiency gain for bare-metal GPU cluster operators.
[Source](https://lambda.ai/blog/unbox-one-of-nvidias-first-co-packaged-optics-samples-with-lambda)

**6. 🔬 IBM Unveils Sub-1nm Nanostack Transistor Technology — Building Chips Taller, Not Smaller**
IBM revealed its nanostack transistor architecture, designed to drive chip construction into the sub-1nm era (targeting the 2030s). Instead of just shrinking transistors laterally, nanostack builds upward via wafer stacking — a fundamentally different approach to chip manufacturing that could reshape server CPU and accelerator design in the next decade.
[Source](https://www.servethehome.com/ibm-outlines-sub-1nm-nanostack-transistor-technology/)

## 🔷 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🔴 ARM Server CPU War Heats Up** — This week saw Qualcomm Dragonfly C1000 entering the ring alongside NVIDIA Vera/GB10 benchmarks, AMD EPYC Venice, and continued Graviton momentum. ARM-based bare-metal options are multiplying fast, with performance now competitive with x86 in server workloads. BMaaS providers should expect more ARM instance types within 12 months.

- **🔌 Co-Packaged Optics Goes Mainstream** — Lambda's CPO switch unboxing confirmed that CPO is not a lab experiment — it's deploying into production clusters with concrete power savings (3.05 kW/switch). This is the biggest change in GPU cluster networking since NVLink and will reshape data center power and cooling architecture.

- **⚡ DPU-Powered Bare-Metal Is the Next BMaaS Evolution** — Lambda's DPU-driven Bare Metal Instances represent a new paradigm: cloud-class API lifecycle management on truly unvirtualized hardware. This pattern could make traditional IPMI/PXE-driven dedicated servers obsolete in the BMaaS segment within 2-3 years.

- **🧠 Custom AI Silicon Fever Continues** — OpenAI's Jalapeño (with Broadcom) joins an increasingly crowded field of custom inference accelerators from Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium/Inferentia), Microsoft (Maia), and others. For bare-metal operators, the question shifts from "which GPU?" to "which custom silicon ecosystem to bet on?"

- **🌡️ Liquid Cooling Is No Longer Optional** — Liquid-cooled busbars at 800V DC from Wiwynn/TE Connectivity demonstrates that the entire power delivery chain is being redesigned for AI-density racks. Air-cooled infrastructure is hitting hard limits, and the transition to liquid cooling — from chip to busbar — is becoming a prerequisite for GPU cluster deployment.
