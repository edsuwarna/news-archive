## 2. 🧠 Tensordyne Napier: 3nm AI Processor with Logarithmic Math
Tensordyne announced **Napier**, a 3nm TSMC AI inference processor with **138 billion transistors** and a radically different math architecture — replacing traditional multipliers with logarithmic additions. The chip delivers **2.1 petaflops per die**, 256MB of on-chip SRAM (5× Blackwell's SRAM), 144GB of HBM3E, and a rack-scale **TDN72** system with 72 nodes, 68 petaflops, and 42TB of HBM. Tape-out is complete; systems are targeting a 2027 roadmap. The big question: can a new numerical approach survive real-world model porting and accuracy testing?

## 4. ☁️ Lambda Launches Bare Metal Instances — API-Driven BMaaS on GB300 NVL72
Lambda introduced **Bare Metal Instances**, combining direct hardware access with cloud-grade API lifecycle management. No hypervisor, no third-party abstraction — just direct CPU, memory, GPU, disk, and TPM access. The architecture uses **NVIDIA BlueField DPUs** in zero-trust mode for provisioning, network identity, and storage volume management, with a BMC handling hardware monitoring and out-of-band boot sequencing. First available on **NVIDIA GB300 NVL72 Superclusters**. This is a direct shot across the bow of virtualized GPU cloud providers — bare-metal performance with API-driven ops.

🔗 [Read more on Lambda Blog](https://lambda.ai/blog/lambda-bare-metal-instances)

## 5. 🔦 Lambda Unboxes NVIDIA's First Co-Packaged Optics InfiniBand Switch
Lambda took delivery of one of NVIDIA's first **Quantum-X InfiniBand Photonics Q3450-LD** CPO switches and published the unboxing. The switch saves **3.05 kW per unit** vs. standard pluggable-optics switches — recovering enough power for **+470 GPUs** in a 10,368-GPU cluster. CPO eliminates roughly **655,000 discrete transceiver modules** at 128K-GPU scale, removing a massive class of failure points. The 4U, liquid-cooled, 115.2 Tb/s switch is designed to slot into the same busbar-powered, liquid-cooled infrastructure as the GB300 NVL72 racks.

## 📈 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **🔥 BMaaS hits its stride:** Lambda's Bare Metal Instances launch marks a new phase for bare-metal-as-a-service — API-driven, DPU-orchestrated, no hypervisor compromise. The convergence of cloud UX with bare-metal performance is the dominant theme this cycle.
- **⚡ Co-packaged optics go production:** NVIDIA's CPO switches entering Lambda's infrastructure signals that the networking power wall is being addressed head-on. Expect CPO to become table stakes for large GPU clusters within 2–3 years.
- **🔩 Hardware diversity accelerates:** From Intel Xeon 6 DPUs on PCIe cards (Senao) to logarithmic-math AI processors (Tensordyne) to liquid-cooled 102Tbps switches (HPE Juniper), the bare-metal ecosystem is seeing an explosion of specialized silicon and form factors.
- **🏗️ Enterprise colocation → AI infrastructure:** Equinix's partnership with Verizon shows that traditional colocation providers are repositioning as AI infrastructure hubs — with software-defined networking, liquid cooling readiness, and direct cloud on-ramps.
- **💻 The GPU bare-metal gold rush:** Every major news item this cycle has an AI/HPC GPU angle. Bare-metal infrastructure is being rebuilt from the ground up for GPU-centric, rack-scale, liquid-cooled deployments — the era of the general-purpose CPU server is yielding to purpose-built AI factories.
